Why EarnIt Exists

I built this for my own kids.
Then realized every family needs it.

"I'm a tech founder building a company. I had to make hard decisions that created a financial gap for our family — but my two incredibly talented kids just made their competitive teams in soccer and dance. I panicked."

I'm Debbie Garcia — a mom of two, a tech entrepreneur, and someone who knows firsthand that money doesn't stretch as far as it used to. When Max (13) and Maisie (9) both made their teams this year, I did what any parent does: I made a budget. And then I thought — these kids should have skin in the game.

They were already doing chores — emptying the dishwasher, feeding the dog. But how could I monetize the extra work? The stuff that goes beyond being a family member? I started researching how kids who earn money relate to it differently. They spend more carefully. Save more intentionally. Give more generously.

So I built an internal family app for us to use. Expectations (free chores). KidGigs (paid jobs). And every dollar earned splits three ways: Spend, Save, Give. The results surprised even me.

Debbie Garcia, CMP
Founder of EarnIt · Mom of Max & Maisie · Nashville, TN

FAQ

Questions parents ask

What age is EarnIt for?
EarnIt works for kids from about 6 to 17. The app automatically suggests age-appropriate chores and gigs based on each kid's birthday. Younger kids get simpler tasks and smaller fines — older kids get more responsibility and higher-paying gigs.
Do I need to use cash or a special bank account?
Nope. EarnIt tracks everything digitally. When it's payday on Friday, you transfer the money however works for your family — Venmo, cash, or a dedicated kids' account. Use whatever works best for you.

What's the difference between Expectations and KidGigs?
Expectations are free chores — things every family member does just because they live there. Making the bed, clearing dishes, feeding the pet. KidGigs are optional paid jobs that go above and beyond — mowing the lawn, cooking dinner, deep cleaning the bathroom. Kids choose which gigs to take on and earn accordingly.
What happens if a kid doesn't do their chores?
The app has a fine system. If an expectation is missed past 7pm, the parent does it instead — and the kid pays a fine (50¢ for younger kids, $2 for teens). It comes straight out of their pending earnings. No arguing, no lecture — just a real consequence. Just like real life.
Can multiple parents access the same account?
Yes. One login per family means both parents can open the app on their own phones and see the same dashboard, the same gig claims, and the same history. Everything syncs in real time.
Is there a free version?
Yes — EarnIt Starter is free forever for families with 1 kid who want basic manual tracking. The full EarnIt Family plan (up to 5 kids, suggested gigs, auto split, payout flow) starts with a 7-day free trial. No credit card needed to start.
